Session 1: Getting Started

Take time to carefully and prayerfully fill out this questionnaire. Be honest and thoughtful in your responses. After completing, discuss your reflections with Modest.

Relationship History

1. How long have you known Modest?

2. How did you come to know Modest?

3. List three of your best memories from your dating experience with Modest.

4. List three of your worst memories from your dating experience with Modest.

5. List several kinds of things you and Modest like to do together.

6. What place should physical contact and physical involvement play in a serious relationship? What guidelines or standards should be allowed?

7. What place does physical involvement play in your relationship? Do you agree on this? Has this been a source of contention?

8. What place should the Lord play in a couple's relationship? What place does He play in your relationship?

9. What do your parents think about your getting married? What do Francesca's parents think?

10. What should a person tell their intended about their past, sins, struggles, or limitations? Are there legitimate reasons for not sharing?

Relationship Evaluation

1. If you were asked to describe your relationship in one word, what would that word be?

2. What word do you think Modest would use to describe your relationship?

3. What are some of the strong points of your relationship?

4. What are some of the weak points of your relationship?

5. What have you done to strengthen these weak points?

6. What do you think are the most important factors that will make a marriage a success?

7. What do you bring to the marriage that will help make it a success?

8. What do you think Modest brings to the marriage that will help make it a success?

Marriage Expectations

1. What thoughts does the word "marriage" bring to you?

2. What are biblical reasons for marriage? Why do you want to get married?

3. What goals do you have for your marriage?

4. What expectations do you have for your partner (Modest) in marriage?

5. What expectations have you had for Modest? How has she fulfilled or not fulfilled them?

6. What expectations do you think Modest has for you in marriage?

Conflict Resolution Principles

Rate how often you practice each principle (0 = never, 4 = always):

1. When a disagreement arises, I focus on what I should do to correct it.

2. When a problem arises, I am ready to admit I may be part of the problem.

3. I realize I cannot change Modest; she must want to change.

4. I realize that love is not primarily a feeling, but actions to serve and help my partner.

5. I focus my attention on changing my own behavior and attitudes.

6. I practice the Golden Rule (Matthew 7:12) - I treat Modest as I want to be treated.

7. I keep current - I deal with one problem at a time and forgive and forget.

8. I try to maintain a close relationship with God through Jesus Christ.
